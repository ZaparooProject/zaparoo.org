Zaparoo requires [Zaparoo Core](#zaparoo-core) installed on your target device,
plus a frontend app such as [Zaparoo App](#zaparoo-app) to manage it and create
tokens like NFC tags. See the [Getting Started](/docs/getting-started)
guide for more detailed steps on getting up and running.

## Zaparoo App

[Zaparoo App](/docs/app) is the primary
supported frontend for Zaparoo. Manage the Zaparoo Core service, take advantage
of your phone's NFC features to easily create NFC tags and use your phone as an
NFC reader or QR code scanner. All essential app features are available for free.

the built-in [web UI](/docs/core/web-ui), terminal UI, [CLI](/docs/core/cli) and [API](/docs/core/api/) interfaces. Zaparoo is an open
platform that encourages useful and unique ways to access media!

## Zaparoo Core

The [Zaparoo Core](/docs/core) application
runs in the background on your device and coordinates all hardware and software
to make Zaparoo work. The service is built for minimal resource usage, so it
won't interfere with your media playing.

<div>
    <h3 id="arch-help">Device Architectures</h3>
    <p>
        If you're not sure what ARM64, x86-64, etc. means, here is a quick rundown of which download you should pick.
        Be aware that the line for some devices may be blurred, and you will need to check the device or OS documentation.
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>x86-64</strong>: a.k.a. AMD64 or 64-bit Intel. Covers most modern desktop PCs, large gaming handhelds like the Steam Deck or ROG Ally, Intel Macs.</li>
        <li><strong>x86</strong>: a.k.a. i386 or i686. Old desktop PCs. Not widely found anymore but the binary may be compatible with x86-64 devices.</li>
        <li><strong>ARM64</strong>: a.k.a. AARCH64 or 64-bit ARM. Most modern emulation handhelds, Raspberry Pi 4/Zero 2 and newer, current Macs.</li>
        <li><strong>ARM</strong>: a.k.a. AARCH or 32-bit ARM. Older emulation handhelds, Raspberry Pi 3/Zero and older.</li>
    </ul>
    <p>
        You won't damage anything by trying to run the wrong one, so don't worry about just trying a download out if you're not sure. If it's wrong, it just won't run.
    </p>
</div>
